# ·s

`·s` is a [verb](../index.md) suffix marking habitual aspect.

Examples:

> Sak [ranuhweʔ](../n/nuhwe_.md)<mark>s</mark> akotyaʔtawi. *([Ormston 1993](../../texts/Ormston1993.md#ex-1-1) – ‘Sak likes her dress’)*
>
> [Tyvʔtho](../y/yv_tho.md)<mark>s</mark>. *([Ormston 1993](../../texts/Ormston1993.md#ex-1-3) – ‘I plant’.)*
>
> [Yerok](../r/rok.md)<mark>s</mark>. *([Ormston 1993](../../texts/Ormston1993.md#ex-1-3) – ‘She chops.’)*
>
> [Rota?](../i/ita_.md)<mark>s</mark>. *([Ormston 1993](../../texts/Ormston1993.md#ex-1-3) – ‘He sleeps.’)*
