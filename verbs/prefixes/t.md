# ·t·

`·t·` is a [verb](../index.md) prefix marking either:
- a first person singular subject
- duplicative verbs
- cislocative verbs

## Agreement

See also: **[·k·](k.md)**

Examples:

> <mark>T</mark>[yvʔthos](../y/yv_tho.md). *([Ormston 1993](../../texts/Ormston1993.md#ex-1-3) — ‘I plant’.)*

## Duplicative

Examples:

> Waʔ<mark>t</mark>[kahriʔneʔ](../h/hri_.md). *([Ormston 1993](../../texts/Ormston1993.md#ex-1-6) – ‘It broke.’)*
>
> v<mark>te</mark>[wasvʔneʔ](../a/asv_.md) *([Ormston 1993](../../texts/Ormston1993.md#ex-1-6) – ‘It will fall.’)*
>
> [waʔ](../prefixes/wa_.md)·[t](../prefixes/t.md)·ha·[yaʔk]()·[e](../../phonology/epenthetic-e.md)·[ʔ](../suffixes/_.md) Sak [rao·aʔshareʔ](../nouns/a/a_share_.md). *([Ormston 1993](../../texts/Ormston1993.md#ex-1-6) – ‘He broke Sak’s knife.’)*
>
> Waʔ·<mark>t</mark>·k·atát·e·[nak](../n/nak.md)·e·ʔ. *‘I scratched myself’ ([Vancaeyzeele 2025](../../texts/Vancaeyzeele2025.md#slide-2))*

## Cislocative

Examples:
