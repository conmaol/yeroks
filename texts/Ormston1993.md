### 1.1. A brief outline of Mohawk

Mohawk is a non-configurational language. The verb is highly inflected, with an overt marker (either an incorporated noun, or an agreement marker) for each of its arguments. Word order is very flexible, and NPs are freely omitted.

Transcription conventions:
- <mark>u</mark> — high back rounded nasalised vowel (elsewhere transcribe as ‘on’)
- <mark>v</mark> — mid central unrounded nasalised vowel (elsewhere trabscribed as ‘en’)
- <mark>?</mark> – glottal stop
- no marking of stress or vowel length

\[p. 2\]

(ex. 1.1)<a id="ex-1-1"></a>

(a) all possible word orders are permitted

> (i) Sak [ranuhwe?s](../verbs/n/nuhwe_.md) [akotya?tawi](../nouns/a/atya_tawi.md). ‘Sak likes her dress’
> 
> (ii) Ranuhwe?s Sak akotya?tawi.
> 
> (iii) Sak akotya?tawi ranuhwe?s.
> 
> (iv) Ranuhwe?s akotya?tawi ne Sak.
> 
> (v) Akotya?tawi ranuhwe?s ne Sak.
> 
> (vi) Akotya?tawi Sak ranuhwe?s.

\[p. 3\]

(b) the NPs may be omitted

> [Ranuhwe?s](../verbs/n/nuhwe_.md). ‘he likes it’

There is another example in the footnotes to page 2:<a id="fn-2-5-i"></a>

> (fn. 5 (i))
>
> [yenuhwe?s](../verbs/n/nuhwe_.md) ne [athere?](../nouns/a/athere_.md) ‘she likes the basket’
> 
> yenuhwe?s ‘she likes it’

Object nouns may be incorporated.

(ex. 1.2)<a id="ex-1-2"></a>

> (a) [Wa?khninu?](../verbs/h/hninu.md) ne [kanakta?](../nouns/n/nakt.md). ‘I bought the/a bed.’ 
>
> (b) Wa?ke[nakt]()a[hninu]()?. ‘I bed-bought.’

Noun incorporation is quite common in Mohawk; it is limited to direct object (theme) arguments.

The agreement paradigm is extremely complex, involving:
- four third-person genders — masculine, feminine, zoic, neuter
- three non-third persons — first-person inclusive, first-person exclusive, second-person
- three number distinctions — singular, dual, plural.

The paradigm is traditionally divided into three sets: 
- subjective agreements
- objective agreements
- transitive agreements.

\[p. 4\]

Intransitive verbs are marked with either ‘subjective’ or ‘objective’ agreements, depending on their lexical properties.

(ex. 1.3)<a id="ex-1-3"></a>

(a) subjective agreements

> (i) [tyv?thos](../verbs/y/yv_tho.md) ‘I plant’
>
> (ii) [yeroks](../verbs/r/rok.md) ‘she chops’

(b) objective agreements

> (i) [wahoyo?tv?](../verbs/y/yo_tv.md) ‘he worked’
>
> (ii) [rota?s](../verbs/i/ita_.md) ‘he sleeps’

Transitive verbs show transitive agreement, except when they have a neuter third-person argument. Third-person neuter agreement is not marked; a transitive verb with a third-person neuter object will show the same agreement as an intransitive verb with the same subject argument, and a transitive verb with a third-person neuter subject will show the same agreement as an intransitive verb which takes objective prefixes.
